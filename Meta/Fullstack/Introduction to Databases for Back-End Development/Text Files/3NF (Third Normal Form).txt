THIRD NORMAL FORM (3NF)

A table is in Third Normal Form if:

1) It is in First Normal Form (1NF)
All values are atomic.
No repeating groups.

Example violation:
StudentID | Courses
1         | Math, Physics

Correct 1NF:
StudentID | Course
1         | Math
1         | Physics


2) It is in Second Normal Form (2NF)
It is in 1NF and has no partial dependencies.
All non-key attributes must depend on the entire primary key.

Example violation:
Primary Key: (OrderID, ProductID)

OrderID | ProductID | OrderDate | ProductName
1       | 10        | 2024-01-01 | Laptop

OrderDate depends only on OrderID.
ProductName depends only on ProductID.
This violates 2NF.

Correct decomposition:
Orders(OrderID, OrderDate)
Products(ProductID, ProductName)
OrderDetails(OrderID, ProductID)

3) It has no transitive dependencies
Non-key attributes must depend only on the primary key, not on other non-key attributes.

Example violation:
StudentID | StudentName | DepartmentID | DepartmentName
1         | Ali         | 10           | Computer Science

StudentID → DepartmentID
DepartmentID → DepartmentName

DepartmentName depends on DepartmentID, not directly on StudentID.
This violates 3NF.

Correct decomposition:
Students(StudentID, StudentName, DepartmentID)
Departments(DepartmentID, DepartmentName)


FINAL DEFINITION OF 3NF

A table is in Third Normal Form if:
Every non-key attribute depends on:
the key,
the whole key,
and nothing but the key.
