What Is the Interview Pattern?
The Interview Pattern is a prompting technique where you instruct the model to act like an interviewer.
Instead of giving all the details upfront, the model asks strategic questions to gather the missing 
information before generating the final answer.

This pattern is powerful because:
- It reduces ambiguity
- Ensures accuracy
- Produces tailored and high-quality results
- Forces the model to think step-by-step and clarify requirements

** Basic Structure **

1. Role Assignment
You tell the model to act like an interviewer:
- You are an expert interviewer.
- Before answering, ask me 3–6 clarifying questions.

2. Clarification Phase
Model asks questions like:
- What is your target audience?
- Should the tone be formal or casual?
- What format do you need?
- Do you want visuals, code, or plain text?

3. Final Output Phase
After receiving enough information, the model produces the final answer with high accuracy.

Example Prompt:
Act as a senior prompt engineer interviewing me.
Before generating any answer, ask all the necessary questions
to fully understand what I want.
Ask your questions one by one.
After you gather enough information, produce the final output.
