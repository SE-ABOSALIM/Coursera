1) Task Specification
A technique used to tell the model exactly what task to perform.

Examples:
“Summarize this text in bullet points.”
“Reduce this paragraph to 100 words.”
It clarifies the structure and scope but does not ensure expertise or bias control.

2) Contextual guidence:
Text prompts provide specific instructions to the LLM's

Example:
"Write a short paragraph on New York City, highlighting its iconic landmarks"

3) Domain Expertise
A technique where you ask the model to respond as if it were an expert in a specific field.

Examples:
“Explain this like a medical expert.”
“Provide technical details as a data scientist.”
This improves accuracy in specialized and technical subjects, such as medicine or engineering.

4) Bias Mitigation
A prompt strategy used to reduce or eliminate demographic bias in model outputs.

Examples:
"Write a 100-word paragraph on leadership traits without favoring any gender. Provide equal 
examples of traits from all genders"
“Do not favor or discriminate against any demographic group.”
“Provide responses that are objective and fair.
This is crucial for ethically sensitive tasks such as hiring recommendations.

5) Framing
Providing contextual framing to shape the tone, style, or perspective of the response.

Examples:
“Explain this as if you were teaching a beginner.”
“Describe this situation in a real-life context.”
Framing sets the approach but does not guarantee accuracy or fairness.

6) Random Sampling
A technique that increases randomness to produce more diverse or creative responses.
It is not suitable for domains requiring precision (e.g., hiring, legal, medical).

7) Framing With Personal Anecdotes
A technique where prompts include a personal story or viewpoint to shape the response.
Useful for tone/personalization, but not appropriate for objective or technical tasks.
